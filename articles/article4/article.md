## üåä ORIGINS: THE BIRTH OF A COMEDIAN

<div style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start;">
<div style="flex: 3; padding-right: 20px;">

Stanley was not your average goldfish. Born in a pet store aquarium between a grumpy betta and an existentialist
plecostomus, Stanley discovered early that he had a gift for making other fish laugh.

"I remember the first joke I ever told," Stanley would later recall in his bestselling memoir *Scales of Justice: A
Comedian's Tale*. "I swam into the glass and said, 'I should really *sea* where I'm going!' The guppies lost it."

But Stanley's ambitions were far greater than entertaining his tank mates. He dreamed of the spotlight, of bubbles of
laughter erupting from human audiences, of his name in lights (preferably waterproof ones).

His chance came when he was purchased by Dr. Michael "The Molar" Finkelstein for his dental practice waiting room.

</div>
